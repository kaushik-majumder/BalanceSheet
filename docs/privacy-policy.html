Receipt Scanner — Privacy Policy

Effective: 2026-05-14

Receipt Scanner ("the app") is a personal expense-tracking app that scans paper receipts, organizes them by category, and optionally syncs them across the devices of members of a shared household. This policy describes what data the app collects, where it is stored, who it is shared with, and how you can delete it.

Where your data is stored

  • On your device — a local SQLite database (receipts.db) and a small encrypted SecureStore keychain holding API keys and auth tokens. This is the primary source of truth for what you see in the app.
  • Firebase Authentication (Google Cloud) — your email, hashed password (if you signed up with email/password), display name, and Firebase Auth UID. Managed by Google as our auth provider.
  • Firebase Firestore (Google Cloud) — your user profile document, your household document, the household's receipt documents, and pending invites. Access is gated by Firestore security rules so only members of your household can read your household's data.
  • Firebase Storage (Google Cloud) — receipt photos and profile photos. Access gated by the same household membership rule.

We do not use third-party analytics, advertising networks, or behavioral-tracking SDKs. The app contains no ads.

How OCR works

Receipt photos are processed by Google ML Kit Text Recognition (Android) or Apple Vision (iOS), both of which run entirely on your device. Your photo never leaves the device for OCR purposes. The extracted plain text is then sent to Google Gemini (or Cloudflare Workers AI as a fallback) for parsing into structured fields. The photo itself is only uploaded if you choose to keep it attached to the receipt.

Encryption

All data in transit between the app and our third-party providers (Firebase, Gemini, EmailJS, Cloudflare) is encrypted via HTTPS. Firebase Firestore and Storage encrypt data at rest by default. The Anthropic API key you optionally provide is stored on-device using the OS's secure keychain (Keychain on iOS, EncryptedSharedPreferences on Android).

Deleting your data

  • Individual receipts: Open the receipt → Edit → Delete.
  • Your entire account and all cloud data: Settings → Account → Delete account. This deletes your Firebase Auth user, removes you from your household's memberUids (or deletes the household entirely if you were the only member), deletes your user profile document, deletes any pending invites addressed to you, and wipes the local SQLite database and SecureStore keychain on the device. If you were a member of a shared household, the other members keep the receipts they saved while you were sharing — your scans remain with the family.
  • Uninstalling the app: Removes only the on-device data. Your cloud data (Firestore docs, Firebase Storage photos, Firebase Auth user) remains until you also run "Delete account" from inside the app on another device, or you contact us to request manual deletion.

Children's privacy

The app is not intended for users under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the app, contact us and we will delete it.

Changes to this policy
